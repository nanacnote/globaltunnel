globaltunnel (a fork of localtunnel)
      

      
        globaltunnel allows you to easily share a web service on your local 
        development machine without messing with DNS and firewall settings.
  
        globaltunnel will assign you a unique publicly accessible url that 
        will proxy all requests to your locally running webserver.
  
        Start a webserver on some local port (eg http://localhost:8000) 
        and use the command line interface to request a tunnel to your 
        local server:
  
        gt --host http://example.tld:1234 --port 9000 
  
        You will receive a url, for example 
        http://assigned-random-subdomain.example.tld:1234, that you can 
        share with anyone for as long as your local instance of lt remains 
        active. Any requests will be routed to your local service at the 
        specified port
